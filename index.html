EBITTYSCHWINNIE

Self sufficiency on two wheels

I grew up in a Big 10 college town in Central Illinois; flat, beautiful grids as far as the bike could ride.

My dad would gear up in spandex and he would ride with me on my 16" bike with training wheels along the shoulder of the country road we lived on. My mom backed over the training wheel on my bike and I refused to ride again until I was 11 years old.

When the rubber really hit the road

Riding my bike let me leave the neighborhood and I didn't have to pay to ride the bus or ask for a ride. I rode a bike for my main mode of transportation until I was 32. After riding as a commuter for my whole life in Chicago and New York City, I started bike camping when I moved to Oregon in 2013. Who knew biking could be for fun?

And also the dirt!

I have been working on riding a bike for fun now, like cyclocross and mountain biking!

My stable

How many bikes is too many bikes? Almost laughable question! I own five bikes currently and am avoiding buying a car as long as possible.

Favorite ride

My current favorite ride is my Surly Ogre, but my heart will always belong to my Schwinn World Sport.
